About Mysore

The beautiful city in India’s South-western state, Karnataka, Mysore reminiscence the great warrior – Tipu Sultan. The name of the city is derived from the demon name Maheshasur who once ruled here and was killed in a fierce battle with Chamundi. One of the cleanest cities in India, Mysore boasts of tree-lined boulevards alongside its wide roads. The city is also a home to intricate handicrafts. The doors of the Diwan-e-Khaas are the finest example of ivory embedded rosewood. Mysore is also popular due to Mysore Pak (Indian Sweet) and Mysore Silk.

Rent a cab from Bangalore to Mysore

Make your trip convenient and comfortable with outstation cabs to Mysore. Get onto NH209 for a fun-filled road trip to the city of palaces. Make your first stopover at Channapatna. This place is quite popular due to its brightly colored wooden toys coated in lacquer. Continue on the same national highway for some time, and you would come across a giant board announcing your entry to Srirangapatna. If you are a history admirer, do pull over here to see ruins of Tipu Sultan’s Palace, the place where Tipu Sultan’s body was found on the battlefield, etc. The place also has Rangaswamy Temple. If you are among the nature enthusiasts, take the steep right branch road after exiting Srirangapatna. The road takes you straight to Brindavan (KRS) and Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary. If you do not wish to visit these sanctuaries, you can take the Outer Ring Road from Srirangapatna that crosses Mysore Highway. The Mysore City Centre is 6 Km from this point.
